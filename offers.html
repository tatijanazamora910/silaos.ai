The build process

How SilaOS gets built — in order.

We follow a specific sequence because order matters. Blueprint before prompting. Testing before launch. One stable workflow before the next.

1

Blueprint first

Before any AI runs, we build the doctrine. Services, pricing, policies, tone, scripts, escalation rules. The AI runs from the Blueprint — not from guesswork. This is always the foundation.

2

Workflow design

We define the workflows SilaOS should run: what triggers them, what they collect, what decisions they make, when they escalate to a human. Deterministic logic before AI behavior.

3

Agent layer

The first agent is narrow and specific — usually Front Office Intake. Limited tools, strict permissions, tight escalation rules. We don't build a general-purpose AI on day one.

4

Dashboard visibility

Before anything goes live, the Dashboard is built. You need to see what SilaOS is doing. Without visibility, you can't trust the system — and neither can we.

5

Studio testing

Every scenario gets run in Studio before it touches a real customer. Pricing questions. Objections. Edge cases. After-hours emergencies. We prove it works before it's live.

6

Launch with guardrails — then expand

We launch the first workflow and watch it run. Only after it's stable do we add the next one. The OS grows in layers — each one proven before the next is built.

Common questions

What people ask before they start.

Do I need to replace my current tools?
No. SilaOS installs alongside what you already use — your calendar, your CRM, your phone system. It fills the gaps rather than replacing infrastructure that's already working.
What if I'm not technical?
You don't need to be. We handle the technical setup. Your job is to know your business — pricing, protocols, how you handle edge cases. We build the system around that knowledge.
How long does a build take?
Most builds take 4–8 weeks from assessment to first live workflow, depending on complexity. We move as fast as the scope allows — but we don't rush past testing.
What happens if I don't want the retainer?
The system stays. What was built in your accounts remains yours. The retainer is optional — it's for clients who want ongoing optimization, monitoring, and expansion. Not for clients who want to run it independently.
Do my customers know they're talking to AI?
Yes — always. SilaOS is honest about being AI. What customers experience is a fast, professional response that sounds like your business. That transparency is what builds trust and makes the system work long-term.
Is the assessment just a sales call?
No. It's a paid engagement that produces real deliverables — your operating map, revenue-leak analysis, draft Blueprint structure, and a build scope. You leave with something useful whether you move forward or not.
